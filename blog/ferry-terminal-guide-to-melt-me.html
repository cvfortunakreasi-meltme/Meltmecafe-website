2 Minutes from Harbour Bay Ferry Terminal: Your First (or Last) Stop in Batam

A short walk, a halal-certified dessert space, and the easiest way to start or end your Batam day trip.

If you've just stepped off the ferry from Singapore's HarbourFront or Johor Bahru's Puteri Harbour, chances are you're hungry, a little warm, and not sure where to go first. Good news — you don't have to go far. Melt Me Dessert & Coffee Space is roughly a 2-minute walk from Harbour Bay Ferry Terminal, right at Citywalk Harbour Bay No. 8-10, making it one of the easiest stops to slot into a Batam day trip itinerary.

Getting There from the Terminal

1
Exit Harbour Bay Ferry Terminal through the main arrival hall and head toward Citywalk Harbour Bay — it's the shopping and dining strip directly connected to the terminal complex.
2
Walk along Citywalk Harbour Bay; Melt Me is located at No. 8-10, easy to spot with its signature cream-and-gold storefront.
3
No need for a taxi, ojek, or grab — this is a walkable stop, which makes it ideal if you're short on time between ferry schedules.
Tip for first-timers: If you're only in Batam for a few hours (a common day-trip pattern for Singapore and JB visitors), Melt Me works well as either your very first stop after landing or your last stop before heading back to the terminal for your return ferry.

Why Tourists Choose Melt Me as Their Batam Starting Point

  • Halal-certified — a reassurance many Singaporean and Malaysian visitors specifically look for when eating in Batam.
  • No transport hassle — save your time and travel budget for destinations further into the city.
  • Air-conditioned comfort — a welcome break from the humidity right after disembarking.
  • Free Wi-Fi and charging — useful if you need to sort out local SIM cards, e-money, or ride bookings before heading further into Batam.

What to Order

Melt Me is best known for the OG Cheezmelt, our take on the Basque burnt cheesecake — rich, slightly caramelized on top, and soft in the center. It pairs well with a V60 pour-over or one of our espresso-based drinks if you want something to wake you up before exploring the rest of Batam.

This July, we've also introduced several new items worth trying on your first visit, including Ube Cheezmelt, Dubai Berry Crush, and Pistachio Cheesecup — a good mix if you're traveling with a group and want to share a few different flavors.

Planning Your Batam Day Trip Around Melt Me

Many Singapore and JB visitors structure their Batam trip loosely around the ferry schedule — arrive, explore for a few hours, then head back before the last sailing. Starting with coffee and dessert at Melt Me gives you a comfortable spot to regroup, check maps, or wait for the rest of your group before heading further into the city.
